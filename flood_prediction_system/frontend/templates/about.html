About the Project

Built to make rainfall data actionable

FloodSense exists to close the gap between raw rainfall figures and the decisions that depend on them — turning historical monsoon patterns into a live, region-specific risk signal.

The Data

Rainfall and river-level records spanning 1980–2023 across 10 Indian meteorological subdivisions, capturing monsoon-driven seasonal patterns and year-to-year climate variability.

The Models

A classifier predicts whether a flood is likely; a regressor projects the actual river water level, so results are both categorical and quantitative.

The Purpose

A decision-support layer for communities, planners, and responders — supplementing, never replacing, official meteorological warnings.

Known Limitations

Predictions are based on historical statistical patterns, not live satellite or sensor feeds. Accuracy varies by region and rainfall extremity, and the model has not been validated against every real-world flood event. Treat results as one input among several, not a substitute for official flood alerts.
